References

Link/Title Summary
Eliminate Texture Confusion: Bump, Normal and Displacement Maps Blog that presents a simple explanation for each type of map. Good for introducing the maps.
Simulation of Wrinkled Surfaces James Blinn's original paper on bump mapping, where (illusory) wrinkled surfaces are made via surface normal vector perturbation.
Efficient Anti-Aliased Bump-Mapping Paper on techniques involved with bump mapping.
Fitting Smooth Surfaces to Dense Polygon Meshes Original paper on normal mapping.
Displacement Mapping on the GPU -- State of the Art Displacement mapping paper.
Factoring 3-D Image Warping Equations into a Pre-Warp Followed by Conventional Texture Mapping The original paper introducing relief mapping by Oliveira and Bishop.
Relief Texture Mapping Discusses the concept of Relief Texture mapping (texture extended with an orthogonal displacement per texel) which is a 2 step process: First the texture is converted to an ordinary texture using 1-D forward transform and then secondly it is mapped on to the polygon using standard texture mapping.
Real-Time Relief Mapping on Arbitrary Polygonal Surfaces Presents a new method of mapping relief textures onto polygons using two depth layers (dual-depth relief textures).
Practical Parallax Occlusion Mapping For Highly Detailed Surface Rendering A SIGGRAPH presentation giving an overview of Parallax Occlusion Mapping (POM) and a comparison with other techniques. They propose an improved implementation of POM.
Practical Parallax Occlusion Mapping with Approximate Soft Shadows for Detailed Surface Rendering A paper corresponding to the above SIGGRAPH presentation on the same topic.
Horizon mapping: shadows for bump-mapped surfaces A paper that describes the procedure behind horizon mapping, which adds shadows to bump-maps.
Interactive Horizon Mapping A paper about Interactive Horizon Mapping, which is an extension of Horizon mapping and self-shadowing.
